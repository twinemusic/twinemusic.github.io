About

Known for their wounded country and barrelling noise rock, Twine return with their new EP Deer in the Headlights (out November 7). This bracing three-song release sees frontman Tom Katsaras sharpening the daggers of his songwriting for piercing tales of intimacy, regression and fear. Mixed by Alex Farrar (Wednesday, MJ Lenderman, Hotline TNT) and mastered by Greg Obis (MJ Lenderman, Duster, Dutch Interior), the EP stands as a triumphant swan song — the final recording whilst the band was still situated in Adelaide, involving members of the band’s foundational Adelaide line-up: Matt Schultz (Guitar) and Thea Martin (Violin), as well as new members Luka Kilgarif (Bass) and Harry Starick (Drums) — as Katsaras transitions to Melbourne At first, title track “Deer in the Headlights” plays as a fingerpicked acoustic love ballad in the vein of John Fahey — “Darling I want to be open / Up from the inside,” Katsaras pleads, his quivering vocals unraveling into bursts of disordered guitars and squealing violins. The second track, “Faith of Crisis”, riffs on the phrase “crisis of faith” — an unruly and explosive account of family, shame and adolescence. EP closer “Out the Same Way” chronicles Katsaras’ move and further probes the fallacies of personal progress — “The wind will clip my wings / Out the same way I came in.” Across these songs runs the image of an unrepentant, furious dog — barking, collared and running backwards. Formed in Adelaide in 2021, Twine began as a solo outlet for Katsaras’ experiments in brash, country-inflected rock. Deer in the Headlights follows the Australian Music Prize-nominated debut New Old Horse (2024), a tender yet eruptive exploration of identity and wayward relationships. Known for their exhilarating live shows, Twine have supported Unwound (USA), The Hard Quartet (USA), Mclusky (UK), and have played sold-out shows across Australia and New Zealand, with festival appearances at Laneway 2025, Camp A Low Hum, Bigsound, and SXSW Sydney..
